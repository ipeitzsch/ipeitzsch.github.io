Bio

I am graduate from Stony Brook University, where I majored in computer science and mathematics. Currently I am pursuing a PhD in electrical and computer engineering at University of Pittsburgh. My research interests include low level systems, space computing, high performance computing, and alternative machine learning methods.

In the past, I worked on the Stony Brook Assembler and Run-time Simulator (STARS). STARS is a MIPS Assembly simulator that is inspired by the MARS simulator. Additionally, I have worked with the Universities Space Research Association (USRA) and NASA to aid with making testing simulation software for NASA's Independent Verification & Validation Facility in Fairmont, WV. I have also done research at Auburn University's REU Site on Smart UAVs where I helped develop and test a new method of making 3D scans of environments using UAVs and LiDAR. Additionally, I worked with Stony Brook University's BME Department to make 2 Android Apps that were both focused on health tracking. My team and I presented a poster for one of the apps at the NEBEC 2018 conference at Drexel University.
